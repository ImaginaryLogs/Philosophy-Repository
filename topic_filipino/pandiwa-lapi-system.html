Types of Verbs:

Abstract:

Filipino has a unique system of implementing trigger in verbs. Trigger gives emphasis on relationship of both the actors and/or the objects to the verb. It can denote who caused the action, who is affected, where it is directed, who is benefited, and much more.

There are some terms that are present in linguistical dictionary


Content:

Active: Actor type

This type indicates that the focus of the sentence is causing the action (verb).

    Types

  1. -UM-

    This infix denotes a specific / perfective aspect.

    Root word Actor focus word Sentence
    takbo tumatakbo Tumakbo ang bata.
    luto lumuto Lumuto ang ina ng pagkain.

  2. MAG-, NAG-

    This affix denotes a complete / perfect aspect.

    Root word Actor focus word Sentence
    luto magluto Magluto ka na ng pagkain.
    laro maglaro Maglaro si Bella sa palaruan.

  3. MA- , NA-

    Infrequently done, but some specific actions use this type of affix. This prefix denotes a perfect aspect

    Root word Actor focus word Sentence
    tulog matulog Natulog ka muna.
    ligo maligo Naligo na siya.
